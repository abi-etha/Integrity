The problem

HR protects the company. That's not a bug — it's the design.

Human resources reports to the company. Its budget, headcount, and leadership depend on the company. When an employee reports abuse, HR's institutional incentive is to protect the organization — not investigate it. This is a structural failure baked into every standard HR model.

The result: abuse goes undocumented, retaliation compounds liability, and boards are the last to know — and the first to be held accountable. 99% of Fortune 500 companies have paid out at least one harassment or discrimination settlement since 2000. That is not a statistic. That is your entire peer group pre-qualified.

"The top 10 discrimination class action settlements totaled $356.8M in 2024, $762M in 2023, and $597M in 2022. The trajectory is not improving. 34% of corporate counselors reported an increase in employment disputes in 2024 — with discrimination and harassment as the fastest-growing concern."

The bigger picture

There are two types of companies Integrity serves.

The first type already knows.

They have seen the complaints. They have read the HR summaries. They have made the call to protect the company and gone home that night feeling it. They do not lack integrity — they lack infrastructure.

For them, the pitch is permission.
The second type doesn't know yet.

But the data does. Wynn Resorts paid $70M. Google paid $50M. Disney settled 7,000 claims for $43M. By the time they find out the hard way, the headline has already written itself.

For them, the pitch is prevention.

Make integrity the path of least resistance.

Integrity does not presume to teach values. It assumes most leaders already hold them. The real tragedy is not that bad actors exist — it is that capable, often well-intentioned leaders are placed inside systems that punish foresight and reward short-term protection.

Our role is to redesign the decision environment. Truth cheaper than concealment. Accountability more rational than cover-up. Doing right by people strengthening — rather than threatening — organizational resilience.

Integrity gives leadership a legitimate, structured path to get the truth without putting themselves in the crossfire. For every board that has ever wanted to do the right thing and had no infrastructure to do it with — Integrity is that infrastructure.

We are not building a punisher. We are building aligned courage infrastructure.
