The problem

HR protects the company. That's not a bug — it's the design.

Human resources reports to the company. Its budget, headcount, and leadership depend on the company. When an employee reports abuse, HR's institutional incentive is to protect the organization — not investigate it.

This is not a people problem. It is a structural failure baked into every standard HR model. The result: abuse goes undocumented, retaliation compounds liability, and boards are the last to know — and the first to be held accountable.

"The entities with the most to lose from an honest investigation are the same entities running it. Integrity was designed to break that loop — structurally, not just contractually."
