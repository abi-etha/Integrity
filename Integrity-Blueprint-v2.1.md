## Executive Summary

Integrity is a third-party organizational governance firm that sells liability reduction and reputation protection to boards and audit committees. The product: independent investigations, culture audits, and structural reform recommendations that identify and neutralize systemic HR failures before they become lawsuits, settlements, or headlines.

The client is not the employee. The client is the board — specifically the people with fiduciary exposure when HR fails. Integrity sits outside the chain of command, reports exclusively to the board's audit committee, and operates under strict independence, confidentiality, and anti-capture protocols.

Integrity does not investigate C-suite conduct directly. It investigates systemic failures at the manager and department level. Where findings reveal what leadership knew and when — that is the data doing it, not Integrity's scope. The mirror shows what it shows.

Integrity's role is fact-finding, analysis, and recommendation. The board retains responsibility for final decisions and implementation. This is not a limitation — it is the correct architecture. Accountability lands where authority lives.

Revenue model: retainer-based enterprise contracts, per-investigation engagements, and annual culture audit packages.

## Problem Statement — Systemic Failures in Current HR Models

**The structural conflict:** HR reports to the company. Its budget, headcount, and leadership tenure depend on the company. When an employee reports abuse, HR's institutional incentive is to protect the organization — not investigate it. This is not a people problem. It is a structural failure baked into every standard HR model.

**The consequences:**
- Abuse goes unaddressed or is actively buried
- Retaliation occurs, compounding liability
- Employees escalate to EEOC, attorneys, and media
- Companies pay millions per lawsuit, per settlement, per headline
- Boards are often the last to know — and the first to be held accountable

**The gap:** No standard mechanism exists for independent, board-level investigation of internal culture failures. Integrity fills that gap.

## Core Principles

- **Independence is non-negotiable.** Integrity never reports to HR or any management layer under investigation.
- **The board is the client.** All findings go to the audit committee or full board — unaltered.
- **Systems, not individuals.** Investigations identify structural failures. Bad actors persist because systems allow them to.
- **Integrity's role ends at the recommendation.** The board retains responsibility for all decisions and implementation. Integrity provides no guarantees regarding outcomes. The client assumes full responsibility for acting — or not acting — on recommendations.
- **Confidentiality is absolute.** All participants are protected by contractual and legal confidentiality frameworks.
- **Findings are actionable.** Every report ends with specific structural reform recommendations and a tracked action roadmap.
- **Suppression is irrational.** The protocol makes suppression more costly than the findings themselves — without requiring Integrity to become an enforcement body.
- **Integrity cleans itself.** Anti-capture mechanisms apply to Integrity as rigorously as to any client.
- **Truth is the product. Culture is the outcome.** The goal is not to punish — it is to make truth and corrective action the path of least resistance for every board that retains us.

## Service Architecture

**Tier 1 — Rapid Response Investigation**
Triggered by a specific incident, complaint, or legal filing. Independent investigator deployed within 72 hours. Interviews conducted under confidentiality. Findings and Action & Resolution Roadmap delivered directly to board audit committee. Timeline: 2–6 weeks. $25K–$75K per engagement.

**Tier 2 — Culture Audit**
Systematic review of organizational culture, leadership behavior, and HR effectiveness. Existing organizational survey data analyzed first — gap-filling surveys only where data doesn't exist. Deliverable: Culture Health Report with Culture Risk Index (CRI) and reform roadmap. CRI is a board-level signal, not a ground-truth measure of human experience. $15K–$40K annually.

**Tier 3 — Structural Reform Consulting**
Post-audit or post-investigation engagement to implement recommended structural changes. Policy redesign, reporting structure reform, governance model updates. *Personnel who conducted the investigation for a given client are structurally prohibited from working that client's Tier 3 engagement.* Priced per engagement scope.

**Tier 4 — Monitoring & Early Warning**
Continuous monitoring engagement. Quarterly culture data review, anonymous reporting channel management, Implementation Dashboard tracking prior recommendations (green/yellow/red), and early warning alerts to the board when metrics deteriorate. *Rotation protocol: no investigator works the same client beyond 5 years without a 3-year gap.* $5K–$10K/month retainer.

## Investigation Methodology

**Phase 1 — Intake & Scope Definition** (Days 1–3)
- Board or audit committee formally retains Integrity — contract signed with audit committee, not HR or executive leadership
- Secondary board contact (lead independent director) named in contract at signing for suppression escalation
- Scope defined: specific incident, department, or organization-wide — manager level and below
- Legal hold issued if applicable
- Investigator assigned — mandatory conflict check, no prior relationship with client organization
- Obstruction log initialized

**Phase 2 — Evidence Collection** (Days 4–14)
- Board-authorized data grant executed — HR documentation accessed under board authority before HR is aware of specific analysis scope
- Chain of custody maintained; all data copied to Integrity's secure environment
- Structured interviews: complainants, witnesses, managers, HR leadership
- All interviews recorded and transcribed with consent
- Obstruction log active from day one

**Phase 3 — Pattern Analysis** (Days 15–21)
- HR documentation compared against HR behavior — policy-behavior gap analysis
- Abuse pattern identification across time, department, and leadership
- Retaliation sequence mapping: complaint → adverse action timeline
- Obstruction log reviewed for pattern threshold triggers

**Phase 4 — Findings & Recommendations** (Days 22–28)
- Report delivered to full board audit committee — dated, version-controlled, logged as delivered
- Findings, evidence summary, structural failure analysis, Action & Resolution Roadmap
- Obstruction log appended if pattern threshold was exceeded
- Risk ratings: legal exposure, reputational exposure
- Anonymized outcome summary prepared for employee communication post-resolution

## Culture Audit Framework

Four dimensions measured:

**Power distribution** — Is authority concentrated in ways that create abuse conditions?

**Complaint pathway integrity** — Do complaint pathways reach independent decision-makers, or loop back to the chain of command being complained about?

**Retaliation climate** — What is the historical outcome rate for people who have reported? Do employees believe reporting is safe?

**Policy-behavior gap** — What do written policies say versus what actually happens? This gap is the single most reliable predictor of legal exposure.

**Data approach:** Existing organizational psychological safety data requested and analyzed first. Survey fatigue is real and degrades data quality. Integrity closes the loop: when data produces recommendations, those recommendations are tracked, and employees receive anonymized outcome summaries confirming that action was taken.

---

## Psychological Safety Metrics — Culture Risk Index

Validated anonymous instruments measuring:
- Willingness to report abuse without fear of retaliation (0–10)
- Perceived fairness of past HR outcomes (0–10)
- Trust in leadership to act on concerns (0–10)
- History of adverse action following complaint (binary + timeline)
- Perceived independence of HR from management (0–10)

**CRI is a board-level governance signal — not a ground-truth measure of culture.** It tells the board where to look. It does not replace what they find when they look.

## Suppression Protocol

**Definition:** Any attempt to delay, alter, withhold, or reframe Integrity's findings before they reach the full audit committee — by any party, including individual board members, executives, or legal counsel.

**Stage 1 — Contact logged.**
Any communication requesting changes to findings, delays to delivery, or scope alterations after evidence collection begins is logged immediately. Automatic. No judgment call.

**Stage 2 — Audit committee chair notified** within 24 hours.
Suppression attempt documented and attached in writing. If the chair is the source — escalate directly to Stage 3.

**Stage 3 — Secondary board contact notified.**
The lead independent director named at contract signing receives direct written notification with full documentation attached.

**Stage 4 — Integrity ethics board convenes** within 48 hours.
Reviews the attempt formally. Recommends: continue, escalate, or invoke termination rights.

**Stage 5 — Findings delivered as written.**
Findings cannot be altered by client request after evidence collection closes. Delivered to the full audit committee — not a subset, not through intermediaries. Client refusal to receive is documented and becomes part of the permanent record.

**Stage 6 — Termination with full board notification.**
If the client takes action to prevent delivery, Integrity invokes its termination clause. Upon termination for cause:
- All independent directors receive written notification of the suppression attempt with full documentation
- The board is contractually required to acknowledge receipt and document their decision on next steps within a defined window
- Integrity retains the complete record of suppression for its own legal defense
- The client's indemnification obligation survives termination

*Note: Integrity does not make external regulatory disclosures. That decision belongs to the board. Integrity ensures the board has the full documented record to make it — and cannot claim they didn't know.*

## Obstruction Detection Protocol

Obstruction is not always overt. Sophisticated obstruction looks like fog — individually explainable events that form a pattern only when logged together.

**Obstruction log is active from Phase 1 intake through final delivery.** Every instance timestamped and categorized.

**Logged automatically:**
- Document requests unfulfilled within agreed timelines
- Witnesses suddenly unavailable, on leave, or departed
- IT access issues or system "maintenance" during evidence collection
- Scope challenges arriving after evidence collection has begun
- Personnel changes in departments under review framed as unrelated
- Meeting cancellations or postponements affecting investigation timeline
- Any communication requesting findings delay or alteration
- Legal counsel contact outside agreed communication channels

**Pattern threshold:** Three or more logged events within 72 hours, or five or more across any 10-day period, triggers automatic escalation to Stage 2 of the Suppression Protocol regardless of whether a direct suppression request has been made.

**The obstruction log is a deliverable.** When the pattern threshold is exceeded, the log is appended to the findings report. The fog is evidence.

## Retaliation Prevention & Response

**Prevention — during investigation:**
Contract prohibits adverse employment action against identified complainants or witnesses during the investigation window. Violation triggers immediate Suppression Protocol escalation.

**Response — for the person retaliated against:**
- Financial stabilization: board-funded commitment (not Integrity-funded) covering medical costs from documented trauma, legal fees, and housing stability during the investigation period. Integrity monitors compliance.
- Anti-blacklisting clause: any negative reference or background check response about an investigation participant triggers an automatic board audit and notification. Blacklisting is already illegal — this makes it visible and expensive.
- Anonymized outcome summary delivered to employees confirming action was taken.

**Response — for the person who retaliated:**
- Default response: structured remediation track, not termination. Termination removes the bad actor from the documented system and allows them to resurface elsewhere with a clean record.
- Remediation track: independently certified retraining curriculum, completion required before any promotion consideration, behavioral reassessment required to demonstrate change.
- Promotion hold documented at board level, requires board approval to lift.
- Retraining curriculum authored by Integrity's framework; delivered and assessed by an independent entity with no financial relationship to Integrity's investigation revenue.

**Post-investigation monitoring:**
Tier 4 clients receive a retaliation monitoring dashboard flagging adverse actions against investigation participants within the prior 12 months.

- HR documentation accessed under board-authorized data grant, chain of custody maintained
- Data stored in encrypted, access-controlled systems with audit logs
- Retention: investigation records held 7 years, then destroyed
- No data shared with HR or management without explicit board authorization
- Whistleblower protections contractually guaranteed to all participants
- All reports dated, version-controlled, and logged as delivered — Integrity's documented delivery record is its legal shield against finger-pointing

## Integrity's Anti-Capture Mechanisms

Integrity applies the same governance standards to itself that it applies to its clients.

**Personnel rotation:** No investigator works the same client for more than 5 years without a 3-year gap. Protocol activates when Integrity has 10+ active client relationships — committed in foundational documents from day one.

**Investigation/consulting separation:** Personnel who conduct a Tier 1 investigation for a client are prohibited from working that client's Tier 3 consulting engagement.

**Internal pattern audit:** Senior reviewer monitors whether any investigator's findings for long-term clients show declining severity over time. Declining severity in a stable or growing company is a capture signal, not good news.

**External ethics review:** A percentage of all Integrity engagements are independently reviewed by an external ethics panel with no financial relationship to Integrity. Results published annually.

**Refusal rights:** Integrity reserves the right to decline or terminate engagements where the client demonstrates bad-faith participation. Termination triggers defined in contract before engagement begins.

**No advisory-to-investigation pipeline:** Investigators cannot move into reform consulting for the same client.

**E&O insurance:** Errors and Omissions professional liability coverage obtained and reviewed with employment counsel before first client engagement. Contracts reviewed with insurer in mind.

## The Mindset Shift — Aligned Courage Infrastructure

Integrity is not only a risk mitigation product. It is a proposition about what kind of organization a company chooses to be.

**There are two types of companies Integrity serves.**

The first type already knows something is wrong. They have seen the complaints. They have read the HR summaries. They have made the call to protect the company and gone home that night feeling it. They do not lack integrity — they lack infrastructure. Integrity gives them a legitimate, structured path to get the truth without putting themselves in the crossfire.

The second type does not know yet. But the data does. And by the time they find out the hard way, Wynn Resorts has already paid $70 million, Google has already paid $50 million, Disney has already settled nearly 7,000 claims for $43 million. The top 10 discrimination class action settlements totaled $356.8 million in 2024, $762.2 million in 2023, and $597 million in 2022. 99% of Fortune 500 companies have paid out at least one harassment or discrimination settlement since 2000. That is not a statistic. That is the entire target market pre-qualified.

For the first type, the pitch is permission. For the second type, the pitch is prevention. Integrity serves both — because the mechanism is the same:

**Make integrity the path of least resistance. Make suppression structurally irrational.**

Integrity does not presume to teach values. It assumes most leaders already hold them. The real tragedy is not that bad actors exist — it is that capable, often well-intentioned leaders are placed inside systems that punish foresight and reward short-term protection. Our role is to redesign the decision environment so that truth is cheaper than concealment, accountability is more rational than cover-up, and doing right by people strengthens — rather than threatens — organizational resilience.

The C-suite is sometimes at the heart of systemic failure. Integrity does not go looking for them. But when the culture audit data lands on the board table, and the board sees what was known and when and what was done about it — the mirror shows what it shows. Integrity stays clean. The truth does the work.

We are not building a punisher. We are building aligned courage infrastructure: the structured permission for leadership to act on what they already know is right. For every board that has ever wanted to do the right thing and had no infrastructure to do it with — Integrity is that infrastructure.
